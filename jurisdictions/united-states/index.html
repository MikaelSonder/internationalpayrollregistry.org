Executive Summary

Payroll in the United States is a structured employer function that converts employment, remuneration and payroll data into lawful salary outputs, withholding outcomes and employment-tax liabilities. In practice, it is a recurring compliance process rather than only a salary transfer. [web:298][page:2]

The function matters because U.S. payroll combines employee withholding with employer-side federal employment taxes. Current guidance explains that the main federal employer taxes are the employer portions of Social Security at 6.2 percent and Medicare at 1.45 percent, while federal income tax is withheld from the employee’s paycheck but must still be deposited by the employer. [web:298]

Federal unemployment is also a distinct payroll layer. ADP explains that FUTA is 6 percent on the first $7,000 of wages per employee, with many employers qualifying for a 5.4 percent credit that effectively reduces the rate to 0.6 percent if state unemployment taxes are paid in full and the employer is not in a credit-reduction state. [page:1]

Recurring reporting and deposit obligations are central to U.S. payroll operations. Current payroll guidance states that Form 941 is filed quarterly to reconcile wages, federal income tax withholding and FICA, while Form 940 is filed annually for FUTA and W-2s are generally furnished and filed by January 31. [web:298][page:1]

Purpose

The purpose of the payroll function is to ensure that remuneration in the United States is calculated, documented, reported and executed correctly, on time and with reliable employer-side controls. [web:298][page:1]

It exists to transform withholding, deposit and employment-tax obligations into a repeatable process with traceable outputs and dependable reporting results. [web:298]

Primary Outcome

Accurate and timely payroll execution in the United States, including correct salary outputs, compliant federal income tax withholding, proper FICA handling, valid FUTA treatment, timely deposits, quarterly and annual reporting, and dependable wage statements. [web:298][page:1][page:2]

FAQ

The FAQ section collects recurring threshold questions in a concise handbook format.

Must an Employer Run Payroll for Employees in the United States?Yes. Employers paying wages for services performed in the United States generally need a compliant payroll process with withholding, FICA handling and reporting. [page:2][web:298]
Does U.S. Payroll Include Employer Taxes?Yes. Employers commonly pay their share of Social Security and Medicare, and may also owe FUTA. [web:298][page:1]
Do Employees Pay FUTA?No. ADP states that only employers pay FUTA and that employers should not withhold federal unemployment tax from employee wages. [page:1]
What Are the Main Federal Reporting Forms?Current guidance identifies Form 941 for quarterly payroll reconciliation, Form 940 for annual FUTA reporting and W-2 for annual wage statements. [web:298][page:1][page:2]
Can a Foreign Employer Trigger U.S. Payroll Issues?Yes. A foreign entity with employees performing services in the United States can face withholding, FICA, FUTA and reporting obligations unless an exception applies. [page:2]
Is Payroll Only About Salary Payment?No. U.S. payroll also includes withholding, employer employment taxes, deposits, quarterly returns and annual wage reporting. [web:298][page:1]
